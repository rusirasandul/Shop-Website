Wind Energy: It is produced through the movement of air, necessitating the installation of wind turbines in areas with consistent wind patterns, such as mountainous regions or coastal areas. This form of energy is renewable and environmentally friendly, experiencing significant expansion in numerous nations. In Spain, it constitutes 25.1% of the total installed power capacity nationally.

Solar Energy: This energy form, also known as photovoltaic energy, harnesses solar radiation to produce electricity through solar panels. It is a sustainable and plentiful energy source, with Spain receiving an average of 2,500 to 3,000 hours of sunlight per year. Importantly, it produces no carbon dioxide emissions into the atmosphere.

close

Geothermal Energy: Geothermal energy is derived from the Earth's internal heat, primarily found in regions with heightened geothermal activity like volcanic areas. This energy source is renewable, environmentally friendly, and limitless, extracted from the Earth's heat stored in rocks, soils, and groundwater.

close

Hydroelectric Energy: This energy is derived from the kinetic energy of flowing water. It is distinct from tidal energy, which relies on the ebb and flow of tides, and wave power, which utilizes the motion of ocean waves. Despite their differences, all three are renewable energy sources.

Biomass Energy: It is regarded as a cornerstone of the circular economy and is produced from dedicated crops and various types of waste, including agricultural residue, forest waste from forest maintenance, agri-food byproducts, and organic waste such as garbage.

close

Tidal Energy: Tidal energy harnesses underwater turbines in narrow passages, while wave energy converters use floats to capture wave motion, both promising vast renewable energy potential from oceans with technological advancements for efficient electricity generation.

close

Wave Energy: Wave energy, a renewable resource, stems from oceanic wave movement. Techniques such as deploying generators on the ocean surface are employed to harness this energy for electricity generation, highlighting the diverse methods available to tap into this sustainable energy source.

close

Nuclear Energy: Nuclear energy, deemed clean due to minimal greenhouse gas emissions and absence of air pollutants from fossil fuel combustion, has faced safety concerns over accidental radioactive releases. However, advancements in regulation, technology, and safety measures have significantly improved nuclear safety over recent decades..
