Renewable Energy Sources

Renewable energy sources are natural sources of energy that are continuously replenished and can be harnessed without depleting finite resources. Unlike fossil fuels, which are non-renewable and finite, renewable energy sources are sustainable over the long term. They produce little to no greenhouse gas emissions, making them environmentally friendly alternatives for power generation.
Examples of renewable energy

    Solar Energy

    Wind Energy

    Hydroelectric Power

    Geothermal Energy

    Biomass Energy

Sustainable Energy Transition

Sustainable energy transition refers to the shift from reliance on fossil fuels to renewable energy sources and the adoption of energy-efficient practices, with the aim of mitigating climate change, reducing environmental impacts, and promoting socio-economic development.


Example:

    A country implementing a sustainable energy transition might set ambitious targets for renewable energy deployment, such as generating a certain percentage of electricity from wind or solar power by a specific date. This transition could involve implementing policies to phase out subsidies for fossil fuels, incentivizing investment in renewable energy infrastructure, and promoting energy efficiency measures in industries, buildings, and transportation.

Energy Efficiency

Energy efficiency refers to using less energy to achieve the same level of output or service. It involves reducing energy waste and optimizing energy use across various sectors, including residential, commercial, industrial, and transportation.


Example:

    Installing energy-efficient appliances and lighting

    Implementing smart thermostats and energy management systems
